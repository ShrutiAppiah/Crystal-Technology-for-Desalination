Overview

Desaline Water with Zero Liquid Discharge

One of the major problems our society faces today is the shortage of fresh water.

Increasing world population, rapid industrialisation, and periodical droughts have resulted in higher water demand throughout the world. To complicate the issue, freshwater resources are diminishing year after year. Desalination plants have become a key components of reliable water supply and guarantee supply even through droughts.

Existing technologies and their shortcomings

The technologies currently used to desalinate water are:

Reverse Osmosis

High pressure water drives through membranes, leaving salt laden Brine behind.

Thermal Desalination

Utilizes energy to heat saline water and subsequently condenses it to pure water.
SHORTCOMINGS

These technologies pose a serious environmental threat.

  Discharge concentrated Brine back into the ocean.
  Affects marine life including the flora & fauna in the marine ecosystem.
  Ultimately disrupts the earth's ecosystem
  Degenerate over time, need to be replaced frequently.

Crystal Technology

Crystal Technology is an environment-friendly alternative to existing desalination methods.

What is Crystal Technology?
Crystal technology intelligently focuses on the target water molecules. It simply uses natural and fundamental chemical principles to adsorb water molecules alone, leaving behind all salts and pollutants.

This state-of-the-art technology produces freshwater irrespective of any pollutant present in the water. It works with sea water, industrial polluted water, chemical effluents, brakish water, community polluted water, etc.

Crystal Technology uses the chemical energy associated with ionic bonds to selectively adsorb water molecules from polluted water, leaving behind rest of the ions in the saline water.
The same logic holds with all types of industrial polluted water streams, chemical effluents etc. It focuses on water molecules only and works excellently irrespective of the nature of the pollutant present in the water.


Operating Process of Crystal Technology
Crystal Technology is used to crystallise sea water at atmospheric conditions to get desalinated water with a yield greater than 60% (research to get increased yields is underway).
In this process, selected additives (polymeric/organic/inorganic) are added as admixtures. These behave like molecular sieves. Sea water is mixed with selected additives, and water molecules are pulled out from sea water to form a crystalline product. During this process, only pure water molecules from sea water are pulled out, leaving behind the rest of the salts in the mother liquor. The crystals are then separated in a centrifuge, and dried and evaporated. Water is obtained as pure water with low dissolved salts in it, with TDS ranging from 10 to 40.

The residue from the evaporator is recycled to recover additives and water, while the mother liquor of the centrifuge is sent to evaporators or evaporating ponds for the recovery of sodium chloride and other salts. All the additives used in the process are recycled. No pollutants are discharged into the sea or nearby streams, realising zero water pollution .
The process can be customised for use with brackish water, and industrial effluents with similar results.

Cost-Benefit Analysis

Crystal technology is a state-of-the-art cost-effective and environmentally-friendly desalination technology with minimal waste.
Recovery
All constituents of sea water are seperated and recovered. Calcium and Magnesium salts are separated in the first stage of operation. NaCl, NaBr, KCl etc. are separated in the third stage of operation. After removing these salts, potable water is obtained.
Compatibility
This state-of-the-art technology produces freshwater irrespective of any pollutant present in the water. It works with sea water, industrial polluted water, chemical effluents, brakish water, community polluted water, etc.
Sustainability
No liquid effluents are discharged into the sea as sea water is entirely separated into its constituents. Salt corrosion is minimal with Crystal Technology. Since the end product has a low TDS with no traces of additives, it can be used in agriculture, industries, and consumption.
Cost-effective
The process does not use any membranes. This accounts for the minimal capital cost of the project. Consequently, no pre-treatment of brine is required, thus reducing the capital cost and operating cost significantly.
